## Decision: Revive Old LLC vs. New LLC

### Revive Old LLC (if still active or recently suspended)
**Pros:** Keep the existing EIN, potentially lower fees, longer business history
**Cons:** May have back fees or compliance issues to resolve

### New LLC
**Pros:** Clean start, name it properly (e.g., "Crea Health LLC"), no legacy baggage
**Cons:** New EIN, new registration fees, starts fresh for business credit history

## Optimized LLC Setup Checklist

### Step 1: Formation
- [ ] Choose state (usually your home state, or Wyoming/Delaware for privacy)
- [ ] File Articles of Organization (~$50-200 depending on state)
- [ ] Get EIN from IRS (free, takes 5 minutes online: irs.gov/ein)
- [ ] Draft Operating Agreement (single-member LLC template is fine)

### Step 2: Banking
- [ ] Open a dedicated business checking account (Mercury, Relay, or local bank)
- [ ] Get a business debit card for all Crea expenses
- [ ] **This is critical for expense tracking** — all business spend goes through ONE account

### Step 3: Tax Elections
- [ ] File as **disregarded entity** (default for single-member LLC) initially
- [ ] When net profit exceeds ~$40K/year → evaluate S-Corp election (Form 2553)
- [ ] S-Corp saves ~15.3% self-employment tax on profits above "reasonable salary"

### Step 4: Compliance
- [ ] Check state annual report requirements (some states require yearly filings)
- [ ] Register for state sales tax if selling to in-state customers (SaaS may be taxable)
- [ ] Get business insurance (general liability, ~$300-500/year)

### Step 5: Assign Assets
- [ ] Transfer Crea IP (code, brand, domain) to the LLC via assignment agreement
- [ ] Update Railway, GitHub, domain registrar to bill the business account
- [ ] Update Apple/Google developer accounts to the business entity

## Timeline
This can all be done in 1-2 weeks. The EIN is instant. State filing takes 1-7 business days depending on the state.
